Beirut

Beirut, the vibrant capital of Lebanon, is a city steeped in history and culture, often referred to as the "Paris of the Middle East." With roots dating back over 5,000 years, Beirut has been a crossroads of civilizations, from the Phoenicians to the Ottomans and French. Its rich past is reflected in landmarks like the Roman Baths, Martyrs' Square, and the National Museum of Beirut. Visitors can explore the city's stunning Corniche along the Mediterranean Sea, the bustling Souks, and the trendy Gemmayzeh and Mar Mikhael districts known for their nightlife and restaurants. The iconic Pigeon Rocks also known as Raouche Rocks are a must-see, offering breathtaking views at sunset. Don't miss the chance to savor Lebanese cuisine, shop for artisan crafts, or delve into the city's art scene in its many galleries. Beirut is a blend of ancient heritage and modern energy, making it a fascinating destination for all.

Byblos

Byblos, Lebanon, also known as Jbeil, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, with a history spanning over 7,000 years. It was a pivotal Phoenician city, famed for its role in the development of the alphabet and its papyrus trade with ancient Greece. The UNESCO-listed Byblos archaeological site features remnants of Phoenician temples, Roman amphitheaters, and a Crusader-era castle. Visitors can explore the charming Old Souk, a market filled with handmade crafts, jewelry, and local goods. The picturesque Byblos Harbor, dating back to Phoenician times, is perfect for dining, boat rides, and enjoying Mediterranean views. St. John the Baptist Church, built in the 12th century, showcases stunning Romanesque architecture. Byblos’ beaches, such as Eddé Sands, are ideal for relaxation or water sports. The city comes alive during the Byblos International Festival, hosting world-class performances in a historic setting. Local restaurants serve delicious Lebanese cuisine, often with fresh seafood and harbor views. Byblos effortlessly blends ancient history with modern charm, making it a must-visit destination.

Baalbek

Baalbek, often called the "City of the Sun," is a treasure trove of ancient history located in Lebanon's Beqaa Valley. This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to some of the most impressive Roman ruins in the world. The Temple of Bacchus, one of the best-preserved Roman temples, and the monumental Temple of Jupiter, with its towering columns, stand as awe-inspiring reminders of the city's grandeur during Roman times. Visitors to Baalbek can explore the sprawling archaeological complex, marveling at the intricate carvings and colossal stone structures. The site also hosts the Baalbek International Festival, where music and art come alive against the backdrop of ancient history. While there, don't miss the chance to wander through the local markets, enjoy traditional Lebanese cuisine, and take in the breathtaking mountain and valley views surrounding the city. Baalbek is a must-visit for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike.
