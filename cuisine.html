Cuisine

The most important meal in arab culture is breakfast.
That is why cold and hot mezze are always on the table in Lebanon.

Manakesh

Manakish (or Manakesh) is a traditional Middle Eastern flatbread, often topped with a variety of ingredients like za'atar (a blend of thyme, sesame, and sumac), cheese, or minced meat. It's a versatile dish, commonly enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack. With its crispy edges and soft, flavorful center, Manakish is a beloved staple in Lebanese and Levantine cuisine, offering a delicious taste of tradition and simplicity.

Shawarma

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from marinated meat, typically chicken, beef, or lamb, that is slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The tender, flavorful slices are shaved off and served in warm pita bread or flatbread, often accompanied by garlic sauce, tahini, pickles, and fresh vegetables. Loved for its savory, aromatic taste, shawarma is a global street food favorite and a satisfying meal for any occasion.

Hummus & Labneh

Hummus is a creamy dip made from blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, celebrated for its rich, nutty flavor. Labneh, a strained yogurt spread, is tangy and velvety, often garnished with olive oil, herbs, or spices. Together, these staples of Middle Eastern cuisine are versatile, healthy, and perfect for dipping or spreading.

Tabbouleh

Tabbouleh is a refreshing Levantine salad made with finely chopped parsley, tomatoes, onions, and mint, mixed with bulgur, olive oil, and lemon juice. Light and vibrant, it’s a healthy and flavorful side dish that highlights the fresh, aromatic ingredients central to Middle Eastern cooking.
